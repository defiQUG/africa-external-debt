Annex A: GRU Legal Framework

This Annex details the legal and technical structure of the Global Reserve Unit (GRU), its asset classes, and the international legal basis for its issuance and use as a sovereign discharge instrument.

Legal Structure

The GRU is constituted as a multi-asset, over-collateralized reserve instrument, governed by the statutes of the Absolute Realms and Organisation Mondiale du Num√©rique, LPBCA. It is recognized under international law, including but not limited to:

  • Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties
  • Geneva and Hague Conventions
  • Montevideo Convention on the Rights and Duties of States
  • United Nations Charter
  • ICC Uniform Rules for Demand Guarantees (URDG 758)
  • African Union Constitutive Act
  • UCC Article 9 (Secured Transactions)

Asset Classes

  • LiXAU (gold) at a 1.48:1 ratio per face value
  • Sovereign bonds
  • Strategic commodities
  • Real estate holdings
  • Blockchain-verified digital assets

Issuance and Registration

The GRU is issued and registered under UCC-1 Filing #20242029991 (Colorado Secretary of State), with blockchain notarization and QR code verification for authenticity.

International Recognition

The GRU is structured in M00, M0, and M1 tiers, ensuring compliance with ICC, UCC, and international standards. Its legal enforceability is supported by the treaties and conventions listed above.

Board-Level Escalation

Any dispute or escalation is to be addressed per the Board Escalation Memo (see attached), with recourse to international arbitration as needed.

Conclusion

This legal framework forms the basis for the lawful, irrevocable discharge of all referenced sovereign debt obligations.
